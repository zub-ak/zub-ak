PhD Student | Researcher in Composite Mechanics
Northwestern Polytechnical University (NPU), Xi'an, China

I am a PhD candidate in the School of Mechanics, Civil Engineering and Architecture at Northwestern Polytechnical University (NPU) in Xi'an, China. My research focuses on Composite Mechanics, with a particular interest in the mechanical behavior, damage mechanisms, and multi-scale modeling of advanced composite materials and structures.
My work aims to bridge theoretical models with practical engineering applications, contributing to the development of lighter, stronger, and more reliable materials for aerospace, automotive, and other high-tech industries.

Research Interests
Mechanics of Composite Materials:​ Stress analysis, failure criteria, and progressive damage.
Multi-scale Modeling:​ Bridging micro-mechanics to macro-scale structural performance.
Damage & Fracture Mechanics:​ Delamination, crack propagation, and fatigue in composites.
Numerical Simulation:​ Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and other computational methods.
